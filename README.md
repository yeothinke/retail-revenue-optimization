# Retail Revenue Optimization Analysis

## Project Overview

This project analyzes retail transaction data from the Sample Superstore Dataset to identify key drivers of revenue and profitability. The objective is to understand customer purchasing behaviour, evaluate the effectiveness of discount strategies, and generate insights that support revenue growth while maintaining healthy profit margins.

The analysis focuses on three key questions:
- How concentrated is revenue among customers?
- Do discounts actually improve business performance?
- What discount levels balance sales growth and profitability?

The results highlight the trade-off between promotional strategies and profitability, a common challenge faced by retail and consumer goods companies.

## Dataset Structure

The Sample Superstore Dataset is a commonly used retail dataset containing transaction-level information including:
- Order date
- Product category and sub-category
- Sales and profit
- Discount applied
- Customer ID
- Region
- Quantity purchased
This dataset enables analysis of pricing strategies, customer purchasing behaviour, and regional sales performance.

## Insights Summary

1. Customer Revenue Distribution
Customer revenue concentration was analyzed to understand whether a small group of customers drives most of the company's sales.

Key finding:
- The top 20% of customers generate approximately 48% of total revenue. 
This indicates that while high-value customers contribute significantly to revenue, sales are relatively distributed across a broader customer base.

2. Discount Impact on Sales and Profitability

Findings:
- Higher discount levels tend to increase order size and sales revenue.
- However, profitability declines as discount levels increase.
This suggests that while discounts can stimulate demand, excessive promotions can significantly erode margins.

3. Optimal Discount Range

Discount levels were grouped into buckets to evaluate performance across different promotion levels.

Results indicate that:
- Moderate discounts appear to support sales growth while maintaining profitability.
- High discount levels often lead to negative profit margins.
This highlights the importance of carefully balancing promotional intensity with financial performance.

4. Category and Regional Performance

Sales and profitability vary across product categories and regions, indicating that product mix and regional strategy influence overall business outcomes.

These findings suggest opportunities for:
- targeted promotional strategies
- category-specific pricing decisions
- regional performance optimization

## Key Business Insights

- Revenue is moderately concentrated among higher-value customers, but a broad customer base remains important for overall performance.
- Discounts increase purchasing activity but reduce profitability at higher levels.
- Moderate promotional strategies may provide the best balance between revenue growth and profit margins.
- Pricing and promotion strategies should be tailored by product category to maximize business performance.
